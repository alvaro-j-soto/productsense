Strategic Context

The Path We're Building

TeamSense earns platform control through a deliberate operational progression. Each step builds trust and data that unlocks the next. The agentic model accelerates this: the AI agent creates breadth (processing every attendance signal), supervisors create depth (handling exceptions through natural conversation). Together they produce operational truth that no HRIS can replicate.

How to Position Each Horizon

Now — Sell with Confidence
Lead with proven value
“TeamSense gives your frontline workers a dead-simple way to report absences via text, keeps supervisors informed in real time, and syncs everything back to your HRIS automatically. Our Employee Assistant lets workers get answers through text — no app needed. We're building the foundation for something much bigger with an early access customer right now.”
Next — Build Excitement
Share the conversational vision
“We're launching a conversational shift start experience — an AI agent texts supervisors at shift start with exactly who's here, who's missing, and what needs attention. Supervisors handle exceptions by just replying naturally. No dashboard, no app. We're also building a points calculation engine where HR uploads their policy and AI configures it — and employees can check their points via text.”
Later — Paint the Vision
Anchor the long-term story
“Where we're headed: when a supervisor confirms someone is missing, TeamSense will recommend the best available replacement based on skills, seniority, and availability. With supervisor approval, the system handles the outreach automatically. Think of it as your shift coverage co-pilot — it learns from every decision and gets smarter over time.”
Roadmap Conversation Guidelines
Do: Share the progression story naturally. Attendance is the wedge that unlocks everything else. Position the conversational model — "your supervisors handle shift start in a 2-minute text conversation" — as the differentiator.
Do: Frame "Later" items as strategic direction, not commitments. Use language like "where we're headed" and "our roadmap includes" rather than specific dates. Points Engine has strong WTP signals you can reference.
Don't: Commit to specific delivery dates for "Next" or "Later" items. Don't position planned features as available today. Don't use internal terms ("TEDS", "SCS", "TIP") with customers.
Don't: Lead with infrastructure or AI model details. Customers care about outcomes — knowing who's missing, filling gaps faster, getting points right. Don't oversell automation: always emphasize supervisor approval.
