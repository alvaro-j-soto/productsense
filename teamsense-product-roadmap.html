How to Position Each Horizon

Now — Sell with Confidence
Lead with proven value
“TeamSense gives your frontline workers a dead-simple way to report absences via text, keeps supervisors informed in real time, and syncs everything back to your HRIS automatically. We're already piloting Shift Start Snapshot with an early access customer — supervisors get staffing counts at shift start, no manual checking required.”
Next — Build Excitement
Share the near-term vision
“We're expanding the Supervisor Control Surface so shift leaders see not just counts, but individual names, can confirm who's actually here, and get coverage recommendations — all before the shift starts. We're also adding skills and certifications so the system knows who's qualified, not just who's available.”
Later — Paint the Vision
Anchor the long-term story
“Where we're headed: TeamSense will detect gaps, recommend the best available replacement based on skills and certifications, and handle the outreach — all with supervisor approval. Think of it as your operations co-pilot for shift coverage.”
Roadmap Conversation Guidelines
Do: Share the progression story naturally. Attendance is the wedge that unlocks everything else. Position "Next" features as a reason to stay engaged and grow with us.
Do: Frame "Later" items as strategic direction, not commitments. Use language like "where we're headed" and "our roadmap includes" rather than specific dates or delivery promises.
Don't: Commit to specific delivery dates for "Next" or "Later" items. Don't position planned features as available today. Don't use the internal term "TEDS" with customers.
Don't: Lead with infrastructure or data model details. Customers care about outcomes (knowing who's missing, filling gaps faster), not how the engine works under the hood.
